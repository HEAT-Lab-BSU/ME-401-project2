1. PROJECT IDENTITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Company Concept:
  A disaster relief company specializing in airship-conveyed, modular
  bio-reactor systems for on-site waste valorization in disaster-affected zones.

Core Technology:
  Hydrothermal Carbonization (HTC) — a thermochemical process that converts
  wet organic and petrochemical waste into hydrochar, process water, and
  recoverable gases under elevated temperature and pressure.

Mission Niche:
  Deploy rapidly to disaster zones and convert locally generated waste streams
  into usable energy or sequestered carbon, without reliance on fixed
  infrastructure.

3. SYSTEM FRAMING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Lens: Process-First
  The system is defined by its process chain. Feedstock selection and
  deployment scenarios are evaluated against process requirements, not the
  other way around.

Primary Sizing Constraint: Energy Self-Sufficiency
  The system must generate enough recoverable energy from waste conversion
  to power its own operations. The energy balance is the spine of all
  design decisions.

Energy Self-Sufficiency Condition:
  E_recovered >= E_reactor + E_preprocessing + E_auxiliary

Waste Streams in Scope:
  - Municipal organic (food waste, sewage sludge)
  - Agricultural residue (crop waste, animal manure)
  - Industrial organic (processing byproducts)
  - Petrochemical / mixed hydrocarbon waste

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PROCESS CHAIN (5-STAGE MODEL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Stage 1 — Intake & Preprocessing
  Function:  Waste reception, sorting, size reduction, moisture conditioning
  Key var:   Moisture content (%), particle size
  Constraint: Energy cost of drying/conditioning must not exceed feedstock value

Stage 2 — HTC Reactor
  Function:  Thermochemical conversion of conditioned feedstock
  Key var:   Temperature (180–250°C), pressure (autogenous), residence time
  Constraint: Must produce net positive energy yield for self-sufficiency

Stage 3 — Product Separation
  Function:  Phase separation of hydrochar, process water, and gas
  Key var:   Separation efficiency, parasitic load
  Constraint: Minimize energy draw; maximize char recovery

Stage 4 — Energy Recovery
  Function:  Heat integration and optional power generation from process heat
  Key var:   Heat exchanger effectiveness, recovery ratio
  Constraint: Recovered heat must close the loop back into Stage 1/2

Stage 5 — Output Disposition
  Function:  Hydrochar end-use (fuel combustion or carbon sequestration),
             process water treatment, gas handling
  Key var:   Char heating value, carbon content, emissions
  Constraint: Output pathway determines whether system is optimized for
              energy output or carbon sequestration

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. KEY ENGINEERING TENSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Moisture Content vs. Energy Cost
   HTC tolerates wet feedstock, but evaporating water is thermally expensive.
   There is a crossover moisture threshold beyond which a feedstock becomes
   an energy liability. This threshold varies by feedstock and must be modeled.

2. Throughput vs. Thermal Mass
   Smaller modular reactors lose proportionally more heat to surroundings.
   Self-sufficiency becomes harder to achieve at lower scales. Scale selection
   is a critical design variable.

3. Hydrochar as Fuel vs. Sequestration
   Combusting hydrochar to power the reactor trades long-term carbon
   sequestration value for operational sustainability. This is a core
   strategic tradeoff the company must define.

4. Airship Payload vs. System Scale
   Although not the primary constraint, the airship platform imposes hard
   limits on system mass and volume that will bound feasible reactor size.
   This becomes a secondary constraint once the energy balance is established.
